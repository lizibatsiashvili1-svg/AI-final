SSIP Ilia State University — GPA Calculation Rule

1. At Ilia State University, GPA (Grade Point Average) represents the quotient of the sum of the products of (GP accumulated within each course) × (credits obtained for that course), divided by the sum of the credits of the courses completed by the student:

GPA = Σ (GP of each course × credits of each course) / Σ (credits of each course)

2. GP (Grade Point) represents the grade received within one course, converted to a 4-point scale.

3. According to the GP system, the grading scale is divided into the following categories:

  1. F  – 0–50 points (0 points, Fail)
  2. E  – 51–60 points
  3. D  – 61–70 points
  4. C  – 71–80 points
  5. B  – 81–90 points
  6. A  – 91–95 points
  7. A+ – 96–100 points

4. Within each course: a grade in category E (51–60) is invariably equal to 1.00 GP; grades in categories D, C, B, A (61 to 95 points inclusive) increase evenly in increments of 1/12 (≈0.08) per point; a grade in category A+ (96 to 100 points inclusive) is invariant and equals 4.00 GP.

5. GPA is awarded within all academic programs at Ilia State University, except doctoral-level studies. GPA is also not awarded for certificate programs.

6. Ilia State University issues only the final GPA, reflected in the diploma supplement. This does not apply to the one-tier Diplomated Physician (MD) program.

7. When calculating the final GPA, fractions are rounded to the nearest tenth.

Footnote: The frozen GP values within the E and A+ ranges reflect that differences between scores within these ranges are not considered significant — any score in 51–60 is equally low, and any score in 96–100 is equally outstanding.
